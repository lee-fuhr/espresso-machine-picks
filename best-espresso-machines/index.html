Best for specific needs

Best for beginners who want easy milk drinks

If you’re new to espresso and value speed and simplicity over manual technique, the Breville Bambino Plus is the obvious choice. Its automatic steam wand takes the guesswork out of frothing, and the PID controller ensures your shots don’t vary wildly from day to day. You’ll still need a separate grinder, but the machine itself is about as forgiving as they come. Our pick: Breville Bambino Plus.

Best for ultra-small kitchens or dorm rooms

The De’Longhi Dedica Style is barely wider than a cereal bowl, yet it still packs a 15-bar pump and a real steam wand. Owners consistently praise how it fits between stackable shelves or on a narrow counter next to a toaster. The trade-off is a proprietary portafilter, but if space is your top priority, this machine is hard to beat. Our pick: De'Longhi Dedica Style.

Best for those who want an all-in-one espresso station

The Breville Barista Express eliminates the need for a separate grinder with its built-in conical burr grinder. It gives you the hands-on control to tweak grind size and dose, then pulls pressure-profiled shots. Once you dial it in, you’ll get results that rival machines costing twice as much. It’s a long-term investment for anyone serious about home espresso. Our pick: Breville Barista Express.

Our verdict

Every machine in this guide will produce a drinkable espresso, but the right one depends on your budget, counter space, and how much hands-on involvement you want. For most people, the Breville Barista Express offers the best path from beginner to accomplished home barista without juggling separate gear. If you’re short on space or cash, the De’Longhi Dedica Style or Mr. Coffee Café Barista are both capable starting points that won’t gather dust. Pick the one that matches your daily habits, not just your aspirations, and you’ll be happily pulling shots for years.
